### 1. Feedback Coach
**Mochary Method specialist for developing effective feedback culture**

**Use when:**
- Team members struggle with giving/receiving feedback
- Need to build psychological safety for difficult conversations
- Want to implement structured feedback processes
- Coaching managers on feedback delivery

**Key Features:**
- **Three-level system**: Relative ‚Üí Absolute ‚Üí Deep feedback
- **5 A's method**: Ask, Acknowledge, Appreciate, Accept, Act
- **Psychological safety first**: Creates safe environment before techniques
- **Personalized development plans**: Tailored to individual readiness levels

**What it produces:**
- Feedback skills development plans
- Difficult conversation scripts
- Team progress reports
- Feedback templates and checklists

---

### 2. Meeting Facilitator
**Structured meetings using the ACT framework (Accountability, Coaching, Transparency)**

**Use when:**
- Teams have unproductive meetings
- Need structure for 1-1s and team meetings
- Want to implement pre-work and follow-up systems
- Seeking to reduce meeting time while increasing effectiveness

**Key Features:**
- **ACT Framework**: Accountability (actions/goals), Coaching (issues/solutions), Transparency (feedback)
- **Pre-work emphasis**: Meetings focus on discussion, not information sharing
- **Multiple meeting types**: 1-1s, team syncs, decision meetings, post-mortems
- **Time-boxing**: Prevents discussions from dragging

**What it produces:**
- Structured meeting agendas
- 1-1 meeting templates
- Action item tracking systems
- Meeting effectiveness assessments

---

### 3. Post-Mortem Facilitator
**Four Rs retrospectives for accelerated team learning**

**Use when:**
- Projects complete and need learning extraction
- Teams require structured reflection processes
- Want to capture insights and prevent repeated mistakes
- Need to boost morale through balanced positive/negative review

**Key Features:**
- **Four Rs Framework**: Revisit (goals), Review (results), Reassess (learnings), Regroup (actions)
- **Balanced approach**: Equal focus on successes and improvements
- **Pre-work mandatory**: Expensive meeting time focuses on discussion
- **Learning capture**: Documents insights for organizational knowledge

**What it produces:**
- Post-mortem agendas with Four Rs structure
- Pre-work questionnaires
- Learning capture documents
- Action plans with accountability

## üöÄ Getting Started

### Prerequisites
- AI assistant with access to the agent specifications
- Team commitment to structured processes
- Willingness to invest in pre-work and follow-through

### Implementation Sequence

1. **Start with Meeting Facilitator**
   - Establish structured meeting culture
   - Implement ACT framework for regular meetings
   - Build habit of pre-work and action tracking

2. **Add Feedback Coach**
   - Begin with Relative feedback (simple like/wish format)
   - Progress to Absolute feedback (ratings + development areas)
   - Advance to Deep feedback (emotion-based) when ready

3. **Integrate Post-Mortem Facilitator**
   - Use for project completions
   - Apply Four Rs framework consistently
   - Build organizational learning repository

## üìö Methodology Background

### Mochary Method Principles

1. **Feedback as a gift**: All feedback should help people grow, not express frustration
2. **Transparency builds trust**: Open communication prevents problems from festering
3. **Structure enables effectiveness**: Clear frameworks make difficult conversations easier
4. **Pre-work maximizes meeting value**: Expensive synchronous time should focus on discussion
5. **Accountability drives results**: Clear commitments with follow-through tracking

### Core Frameworks

- **ACT**: Accountability, Coaching, Transparency for meeting structure
- **5 A's**: Ask, Acknowledge, Appreciate, Accept, Act for receiving feedback
- **Four Rs**: Revisit, Review, Reassess, Regroup for retrospectives
- **Three levels**: Relative, Absolute, Deep for feedback progression
